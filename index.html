01

Information Stokk handles

We try to keep Stokk lightweight. For core play, the app does not describe itself as requiring real-world banking credentials, brokerage credentials, or payment-card details. Instead, the app mainly uses information needed to remember your simulated trading setup and to load supported market data.

When you first use Stokk, the app may store a username you choose and a locally generated player ID. The app may also store simulated account information such as your starting fake balance, current paper holdings, trade history, streak data, unlocked cosmetics or rewards, worker state, and other in-app progress so that the app can restore your experience the next time you open it.

To show live or near-live price information, Stokk requests data from third-party market-data providers. Those providers may receive technical request information such as your IP address, general device or browser details, request timing, and other network metadata that is commonly processed when an app fetches remote content.

If advertising is enabled in a given build of Stokk, ad-related software development kits or services may also process device identifiers, ad identifiers, diagnostics, approximate location inferred from IP, interaction events, and fraud-prevention signals in order to load, measure, or secure banner ads, app-open ads, or rewarded ads.

Examples of information involved in the current experience
  • Profile data: a chosen username and a locally generated player ID.
  • Simulated trading data: fake balance, holdings, trade history, and reward progression.
  • Technical request data: information sent when the app asks remote services for market prices.
  • Ad-related data: identifiers and diagnostics processed by ad providers where ads are enabled.
  • Support communications: anything you voluntarily send us by email.
02

How the information is used

We use information to operate the app users expect to see. That includes creating and restoring your local paper-trading profile, maintaining simulated balances and holdings, showing price changes and charts, supporting in-app reward systems, and making optional features such as share actions or ad-supported rewards function correctly.

Stokk may also rely on technical logs, request outcomes, and provider diagnostics to keep the app functioning, investigate failures, reduce abuse, and understand whether remote requests or ad loads are working as intended.

We may use information to:
  • Run the simulated trading experience and remember your state across sessions on the device.
  • Fetch market data so charts, price moves, and supported assets can appear in the app.
  • Support monetization features such as rewarded or banner ads where included.
  • Respond to support messages and review privacy-related questions or requests.
  • Protect the service by investigating misuse, technical abuse, or operational issues.
03

Sharing and third parties

We do not describe Stokk as selling a user's paper-trading history as a product. Even so, Stokk may involve third parties in order to provide core remote functionality or monetization. Market-data services may receive requests when the app loads prices. Ad providers may process data when ads are requested or displayed. If you choose to use a device share sheet, your device platform may process the content you choose to share.

We may also disclose information when reasonably necessary to comply with law, enforce our rights, investigate abuse, or protect users, developers, or the public.

Third-party categories currently relevant to Stokk
  • Market-data providers used to retrieve supported price and chart information.
  • Advertising providers used to deliver banner, app-open, or rewarded ads.
  • Platform-level sharing tools when you intentionally share app content.
  • Email providers when you contact us directly for support or privacy matters.
04

Retention and storage

Much of the core Stokk experience is currently local-first. That means the profile and gameplay information described above may remain on your device until you clear the app's stored data, use an in-app reset function if available, or uninstall the app. Because local storage is part of how the app remembers your progress, deleting app data may also remove your simulated holdings, history, and unlocks from that device.

Third-party providers such as ad services, hosting layers, and market-data services may keep their own logs, records, or security data for their own operational, contractual, legal, fraud-prevention, and analytics purposes. Their retention timelines are controlled by them.

Your choices and rights

Depending on your device, platform, and local law, you may have choices about how certain data is handled. For example, you may be able to reset local app data, control ad-related settings on your device, disable certain permissions, or choose not to use optional sharing features.

If you want to ask for access, deletion, clarification, or another privacy-related action, contact us at pigeon.software.2012@gmail.com. We may need enough information to understand the request and confirm that we are responding appropriately.

In practice, you can usually:
  • Clear local data by resetting app storage or reinstalling the app.
  • Limit ad tracking through device settings where your platform supports that control.
  • Avoid optional sharing features if you do not want to send content through your OS share sheet.
  • Email us directly if you want privacy help or need clarification on this policy.
