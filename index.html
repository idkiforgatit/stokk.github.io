Privacy Policy for Stokk

Stokk is a paper-trading app built for practice. It lets you track supported market prices, test ideas, and simulate trades with fake money. This policy explains what information Stokk handles, what stays on your device, where third-party services may come into play, and what choices you have.

No real-money trading or custody
Core game progress stored locally
Live market prices from external providers
Ads may use third-party SDKs

At a glance

  • Minimal first-party data Stokk mainly stores your profile and paper-trading progress on your own device.
  • Third-party services exist Live pricing and advertising may involve outside providers with their own policies.
  • Practice only Stokk does not open brokerage accounts, hold funds, or execute real trades.

Overview

What Stokk is, and what it is not

Stokk is designed for learning, testing, and entertainment. The balances, portfolios, streaks, rewards, and trades inside the app are simulated. Stokk is not a bank, broker, investment adviser, money transmitter, or custodial wallet. We do not ask you for brokerage logins, debit card numbers, or other payment credentials in order to use the core simulated trading features.

The presence of real or near-real market prices inside the app does not mean that Stokk can place trades or move assets on your behalf. It is practice software.

Collection

Information Stokk handles

We aim to keep Stokk lightweight. Most of the information used to make the app work is either stored locally on your device or passed through service providers needed for pricing data, platform sharing, and advertising.

1. Profile information you enter

When you set up Stokk, the app stores the username you choose and a locally generated player ID. This helps the app remember your profile on that device.

2. Local gameplay and portfolio data

Stokk stores simulated account values and progress locally, including items such as paper balance, holdings, trade history, streaks, unlocked items, selected cosmetics, worker state, and similar in-app progress.

3. Market data requests

To show current prices and charts for supported assets, Stokk requests market information from third-party data providers. Those providers may receive technical request data such as IP address, request timing, and device or browser metadata.

4. Advertising-related data

If ads are enabled in your build of Stokk, banner, app-open, or rewarded ad features may use Google Mobile Ads or similar tools. Ad providers may collect device identifiers, diagnostics, approximate location inferred from IP, ad interactions, and fraud-prevention signals under their own privacy terms.

  • Stokk may use your device share sheet if you choose to share in-app content or rewards.
  • We may receive support messages or emails you voluntarily send us.
  • We do not currently describe Stokk as requiring real-name identity checks for basic use.

Usage

How information is used

We use information to run the app you expect to see. That includes maintaining your local paper portfolio, loading prices, rendering charts, unlocking rewards, preventing obvious abuse of in-app systems, serving ads where enabled, and responding to support requests.

Operate the paper-trading experience

This includes remembering your username, simulated balance, positions, trade records, and progression so Stokk can reopen in the same state on your device.

Deliver live market views

Current prices, price changes, charts, and supported market feeds require requests to outside market-data services.

Support monetization features

If your version of Stokk includes ads, ad requests and reward completions may be processed through the ad provider to display ads and determine whether a reward was earned.

Improve reliability

Technical logs, warnings, and service-provider diagnostics may be used to identify errors, availability issues, or abuse patterns.

Sharing

When information may be shared

We do not sell your paper-trading history as a product. However, some information may be shared with service providers when needed to run features you use.

  • Market-data providers may receive request metadata when Stokk asks for price or chart data.
  • Advertising providers may process ad-request, device, and interaction data if ads are shown.
  • Platform share tools may process the content you choose to share through your device OS.
  • We may disclose information if required by law, legal process, or to protect rights and safety.
Third-party providers control their own systems. Their handling of data is governed by their own notices and policies, not just this page.

Retention

How long data is kept

Stokk currently stores much of its core profile and simulated trading state locally on your device until you clear app data, use an in-app reset option if available, or uninstall the app. Third-party providers such as ad or market-data services may keep their own logs for their own operational, security, legal, and analytics purposes.

Local app data

Username, player ID, simulated holdings, trade history, streaks, and reward state can remain on-device until removed.

Support messages

If you contact us, we may keep those communications for as long as reasonably needed to resolve the issue and maintain support records.

Controls

Your choices and rights

The controls available to you may depend on your device, platform, and the exact Stokk build you are using. In general, you can take steps such as the following:

  • Change or reset your local app data by clearing the app's stored data or reinstalling the app.
  • Limit ad tracking or reset your advertising identifier through supported device settings.
  • Choose not to use optional sharing features.
  • Contact us if you want to ask about data access, deletion, or policy questions.
If Stokk later adds cloud accounts, sync, social profiles, or competitive leaderboards, this policy should be updated to reflect the new data flows and account controls.

Security

Security, children, and policy changes

We use reasonable steps appropriate to a lightweight app of this type, but no service can promise absolute security. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child submitted personal information through Stokk, contact us so we can review the issue. We may update this policy as the app evolves, including if new features such as accounts, sync, analytics, or additional monetization tools are introduced.

  • Stokk is intended for simulated learning and testing, not real investing activity.
  • Users should avoid sharing sensitive financial or identity data through support channels.
  • Material updates should be reflected by changing the effective date on this page.
